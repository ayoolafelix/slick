# Pitch Narrative

## 1. Problem

Creators who want direct paid access online still rent distribution from closed platforms. They lose fees, branding, customer ownership, or all three the moment they charge for something digital.

## 2. Why now

Solana made low-latency, low-fee wallet payments feel fast enough for consumer software, while Phantom and mobile wallets made those payments usable outside a trading interface. That means monetization can finally behave like product infrastructure instead of checkout glue.

## 3. Solution

A creator sets a price, shares a link, and gets paid the moment content unlocks. Buyers can either unlock once through payment records or hold a transferable access pass that turns paid access into a portable asset. This is not a platform. It is infrastructure.

## 4. Why Solana

The experience only works if confirmation is fast enough that silence during the demo feels impressive, not risky. Solana gives the fee profile, wallet ecosystem, and composability to make paid access feel like a primitive rather than an expensive edge case.

## 5. Vision

Today this is a creator paywall. In three years, it becomes the monetization layer any app can plug into for paid content, paid tools, meterable APIs, and portable access logic. Stripe made card payments programmable. We want to do that for wallet-native monetization.

## Skeptical judge question

Why not just use Stripe and Gumroad?

## Answer

Because those tools process payments inside closed platforms. We’re making monetization portable, programmable, and composable with wallets, NFTs, referrals, and DeFi-native payment logic.

## Metric that matters

The first meaningful traction signal is repeat creator usage: how many creators publish more than one paid drop and how many buyers unlock more than one piece of content through the same wallet.
